YSDN4003 - Weekly process

Week 1

What is your concrete topic?

Neuralink is a neural engineering company developing high bandwidth brain-machine interfaces. The BMI implant produced by Neuralink is inserted into the top of the head through connecting micro-threads in neural pathways and detecting neural activity - specifically the activity know as ‘action potential’ - to allow the user to control Bluetooth enabled devices. The company was founded by Elon Musk in 2016 and has had promising results with recent testing of the Neuralink implants in pigs. I am designing a companion app for the Neuralink implant, The Link, that helps implant users analyze their brain data.

What is the goal of the project?

My primary goal for the project is to design a companion app for the Neuralink implant. The implant is initially going to be used as a medical device to treate conditions such as nerve damage, paralysis, alzheimers, and a variety of other medical conditions.

What do you like about this idea?

I like that there are opportunities to experiment with different kinds of data visualizations. As well, there are a lot of opportunities to create a speculative technology, as the implant functionality hasn’t been finalized.

What elements or factors could risk the achievement of your project?

It is an extremely powerful medical device and requires special attention be paid to the matters of data security. The technology is emerging and unusual to essentially all people, so the app needs to be designed in a way that helps people feel comfortable with using it. As well, the technology is very powerful and can affect your nervous system, so this safety needs to be prioritized within the app.

What's your inspiration for the project?

The future of augmentation technology is super exciting. As COVID has forced us to rapidly digitalize our societies, the necessity of digital and trans-human augmentation - to connect, buy groceries, get healthcare - has rapidly increased. Post-human interfacing is an extremely interesting emerging field in design. As graphic design transformed the internet - through providing GUIs that allowed lamence users to utilize the complex activities on the internet without needing to know how to code.

The basics of Brain-machine interfacing

Neurons communicate through electric signals and stimulate channels within the dendrites through a spatiotemporal synaptic input, it initiates ‘action potential’. The action potentials can be read through the use of electrodes and getting large amounts of information through the use of electrodes to gather it from the brain.

The device they use is a neural implant that will let you control a computer or mobile device. Micron-scale threads are inserted into areas of the brain that control movement. These threads contain electrodes and connects the person to the implant, The Link.

The link processes, stimulates, and transmits neural signals. The threads are super-fine. The implant is charged wirelessly from the outside. The link allows you to control an OS, keyboard, or mouse directly by just thinking about it. It would allow someone to operate things with their brain but also would allow for information to be written into the brain (for example, to restore someone’s sense of touch). Allows people to communicate via text or speech synthesis. Also allows for photography, art, or writing.

The main challenges to link are: scaling up the amount of electrodes they can safely and legally use. Chip electrodes need to be ultra small. Real-time conversion of neural information. As well, current BMI (brain-machine interfaces) only allow for brain stimulation, instead of transferring information to and from the brain.

According to John Thomas, Tomasz Maszczyk, Nishant Sinha, Tilmann Kluge, and Justin Dauwels “A BCI system has four major components: signal acquisition, signal preprocessing, feature extraction, and classification.” (towardsdatascience.com)

Week 3

The project I chose to work on is Bernarda Avila's untitled app for supporting domestic abuse victims

I chose to work on this project because domestic abuse has become even more prominent in the midst of the coronavirus pandemic. Providing digital communities of support for victims of intimate partner violence is especially important right now with the pandemic continuing for an indeterminate amount of time and people being increasingly reliant on online community building.

Heuristic analysis

Heuristic analysis summary

This project has a well-established visual identity, though requires some aspects to be more fleshed out in a way that is supportive of the sensitivities and unique needs of victims of domestic violence.

One of the key decisions to make about the app is whether it is beneficial as a short-term or a long-term tool. For example, is this the kind of tool someone could use if they are in imminent danger? Or is this moreso for getting victims out of domestic violence situations and supporting them emotionally in the long-term? I think that users attach emotions to the tools they use so I don’t believe that this could be both types of app simultaneously.

My hypothesis is that anonymity and data security are extremely important to the success of the this app and that these issues are not thoroughly addressed in the initial concept. As well, names for certain product features are often unclear and would benfit from more specificity.

The app is content heavy so the overall content strategy and curation needs to be thoroughly considered. Currently, it looks like there are specific contributors who can add content to the app and then threads in which anyone can post on it. I think that considering how vulnerable people are in domestic violence situations that we need to consider how people get to interact with threads. For example, if they are in a group for learning more about dealing with substance misuse (by themselves or their partner) then perhaps they only want to see thread comment’s from other people in the same groups as them. This is similar to how Reddit handles threads, by limiting the ability for your posts to be seen by someone unless they are in the same group/subreddit as you or the post was explicitly made public.

I am uncertain if the feature for calling 9-1-1 is necessary to the app’s functionality. As well, it is super easy to call 9-1-1 and I think that could be more detrimental - and potentially deadly - is the police showed up at the home of someone in a domestic violence situation. There should be more intentionality needed to activate the feature, if it is included.
