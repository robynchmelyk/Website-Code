Introduction

Otherworldly is a dramatic, unusual variable typeface for use in audio-visual and editorial environments.

'Otherworldly' is an ultra-contrasted, New Wave font that transforms from ultra-condensed to ultra-wide to wavy characters. The typeface will be used as a supplement to sound-based experiences and will oscillate between the different parameters of the variable font in response to sound. The typeface will be used as a decorative display typeface that is responsive to its environment. The font will look like it is dancing as it transforms between the different ends of the spectrum.

OTHERWORLDLY

TYPEFACE OVERVIEW

The overall feeling of the typeface is dramatic, psychedelic, modern, bold, and unusual.

The font will likely be digitally projected onto a surface, displayed on a screen behind a performer, or used for editorial pages. It will primarily be used at concerts, music festivals, and gallery or arts spaces - largely due to the frequency of video projections and sound-based media at these events and venues. It will use a javascript algorithm and a sound sensor to determine sound levels which would move the font’s shape along the transformation parameter in response.

Design Features

The typeface features dramatically contrasted stroke widths, tight counter space, rounded corners, and ball terminals.

The fitting will be quite tight between the characters because of the semi-condensed nature of the font. The thin stroke letterform will become semi-condensed to keep the overall width of the lines similar as it transforms. The typeface will feature tight counter spaces and closed apertures to optimize the legibility of the font from a distance. The stress axis will be vertical to capture a dramatically modern feeling and will also help the letters to fit together more effectively. The ultra-contrasted nature of 'Otherworldly' makes it especially suitable for editorial design.

Research

The overall tone for the font draws from New Wave and Post-Modern font design aesthetics and typographers.

The concept for the font was developed through researching emerging New Wave, experimental trends in font design. Drawing largely from visual research of social media accounts of global contemporary type designers and Future Fonts (www.futurefonts.xyz), the font was developed to be appealing to an audience with sophisticated knowledge and appreciation of design. The font is also inspired by 'Bodoni' and 'Didot' - which revolutionized type design and created massive movements around developing highly editorial and less strictly legible fonts.
