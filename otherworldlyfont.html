Introduction

Otherworldly is a dramatic, unusual variable typeface for use in audio-visual and editorial environments.

This typeface will be used as a supplement to sound-based experiences and will oscillate between the different parameters of the variable font in response to sound. The typeface will be used as a decorative display typeface that is responsive to its environment. It’s variable parameter will change the typeface between a thin, light, semi-condensed sans-serif letterform into an ultra wide, dramatically curved letterform. The font will look like it is dancing as it transforms between the different ends of the spectrum.

TYPEFACE OVERVIEW

The overall feeling of the typeface is dramatic, psychedelic, modern, bold, and unusual.

The font will likely be digitally projected onto a surface, displayed on a screen behind a performer, or used for editorial pages. It will primarily be used at concerts, music festivals, and gallery or arts spaces - largely due to the frequency of video projections and sound-based media at these events and venues. It will use an algorithm and a sound sensor to determine sound levels which would move the font’s shape along the transformation parameter in response.

Design Features

The typeface features dramatically contrasted stroke widths, tight counter space, rounded corners, and ball terminals.

The fitting will be quite tight between the characters because of the semi-condensed nature of the font. The thin stroke letterform will become semi-condensed to keep the overall width of the lines similar as it transforms. The typeface will feature tight counter spaces and closed apertures to optimize the legibility of the font from a distance. The stress axis will be vertical to capture a dramatically modern feeling and will also help the letters to fit together more effectively.
