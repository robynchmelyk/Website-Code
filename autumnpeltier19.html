'IndigenousNow' Social Movement Campaign

Street and subway poster set and promotional sticker.

The Brief

The goal of this project was to design a multi-media social awareness campaign that addresses and raises awareness of issues of Indigenous colonization in Canada.

Tools

Adobe Photoshop

Adobe Illustrator

Timeline

3 weeks

Designer

Robyn Chmelyk

Business Objectives

To influence working-class Canadians, age 18-35 to prioritize Indigenous issues and to take action that helps create lasting reconciliation and change.

To influence the target audience to see supporting Indigenous communities as more important and to raise awareness of the First Nations' water crisis by drawing attention to the stories and work of modern Indigenous water activists in Turtle Island (Canada).

Rationale

The overall creative direction of the campaign was focused on the work, activism, and voice of current Anishnaabek Chief Water Comissioner, Autumn Peltier. The colours in the design are the traditional colours of the Wikwemkoong Nation - red, green, black & white.

The text content is drawn from a speech that Autumn Peltier planned to give to Justin Trudeau in 2017 - as well as, a copper water bowl. However, Trudeau did not offer her the time to give her speech, which led Autumn to post her written speech online. The 'unspoken' words of Peltier are given a powerful, bold visual aesthetic that is impossible to ignore.

Posters are used because they can be placed in public spaces and are able to affect a much more diverse audience this way. Additionally, the stickers were designed to provide a lasting mark and awareness of Autumn Peltier within public space.

One of Autumn's claims to fame in mainstream Canadian media is through delivering a copper water bowl to Justin Trudeau and to ask him why he continued to support pipeline development. The image features a water bowl and a splashing water fall contrasted with very bold typography and a popular quote by Autumn.

The striking imagery of Autumn dripping in oil-esque paint in her Anishnaabek Chief Water Commissioner regalia is extremely evocative and tells a story through minimal use of text - while broadcasting her name.

This is a Wikwemkoong prayer that Autumn began her speech to Trudeau with. Indigeous communities in Canada have been forced to learn English and French while being discouraged from speaking their native language. This sentiment is inverted through having a poster with almost exclusively Wikwemkoong words on it - forcing the public to engage with Indigenous language.

This sticker was designed to help supporters of Autumn's cause stick her name around their communities and raise greater awareness of Autumn Peltier's activism.
