'Frend' App Design

E-mental health data visualization app with an AI-guided chatbot coach.

The Brief

The goal of this project was to design an effective and feasible mobile app.

Tools

Figma

Adobe Illustrator

Timeline

5 weeks

Designers

Robyn Chmelyk

Business Objectives

To produce an app that visualizes the wide variety of data collected by your smartphone to better inform the user about their behaviours.

To produce a low- to no-cost e-mental health resource that adapts to the user based on their individual needs.

To access the figma prototype, click here.

The Solution

The solution I designed The solution involves automating the labour of mental health support while tracking your data and progress in a variety of wellness-related tasks, such as your physical activity, device usage, mood, and much more. The AI-chatbot acts as a form of digital therapist that can support the user and, ideally, help them ease their chronic mental illness symptoms.

LIVE PROTOTYPE

The Problem

The problem addressed is the privatization of mental health care and the main barrier to individuals accessing mental health support, as outlined by the World Health Organization (WHO); cost. Recent studies suggest 970 million people are affected by mental illness globally - approximately 15% of the total human population - and access to proper therapy can drastically affect the outcome of someone’s condition.

I drew my research largely from the WHO’s report on gaps in mental health care, Mental Health Gap Action Program Intervention Guide It outlines the most common type of mental illnesses and the different symptoms that compose these conditions. My overall research showed that anxiety, depression, addiction, PTSD, and ADD/ADHD are the most common mental health conditions, thus the app needed to address the symptoms of these conditions.

The main goal of the project was to create an interface that would help automate therapeutic labour.

User Research Insights

Elements that are clickable should read more clearly as buttons and that they are clickable.

Use different animations to help the visual flow of the prototype.

Hotspots for buttons need to be made larger as users were having issues clicking buttons.

Text size is too small in some areas of the app and needs to be increased to improve legibility..

The users wanted the ability to go back and change information in the onboarding phase.

Some overlay elements and dropdown menus require a background colour to improve readability.

High-Fidelity Mockups

Dashboard

The dashboard is the landing page for returning users whenever they open the app. Their widget collection is displayed and organized into one space. At the bottom of the page, users are able to search and add additional widgets.

Chatbot Screen

The AI guided chatbot is designed to support users in the actualization of their goals. Whether it be cutting down on caffeine consumption, building healthy life skills, or becoming more active, the chatbot 'Sam' will modify their behaviour to support the user.

Quiz Page 1

First time users will be prompted to enter their details within a digital quiz. This data informs the behaviour of the AI chatbot while also being able to suggest the widgets that would be the most helpful for the user.

Quiz Page 2

Data Tracking Page

The data visualization and data entry tracker is tailored to the individual widget though it provides the user with the ability to enter data - to be visualized by the widgets - and confirm soft data collected by their smartphone.

Add Widgets Page

The user is able to add any widgets that they would like. The user is able to sort through all of the widgets and get additional information on the widget by clicking on 'Info'.

Style Guide

Project Reflections

Maintaining a complex design system can be super complex and adhering to a strict style guide is essential in producing an effective, visually appealing app without having to constantly update a plethora of tiny details.

Telling a clear story using UX/UI design while optimizing the app for what is feasible, functional, and usable is a difficult balancing act!

Next time, I would definitely incorporate illustrations to help humanize the content within the app and make it feel more inviting.
